\chapter{Conclusion and Discussion}
\label{chapter:conclusion_discussion}

\section{Synthesis of Findings}
\label{sec:synthesis}

This study applied Cooke's Classical Model to seven experts' assessments of the Dutch housing market in 2027. Ten calibration variables provided empirical control: experts were weighted according to statistical accuracy and informativeness rather than credentials or self-confidence. Among the three pooled models, the optimized Decision Maker achieved the highest combined score and was therefore used for the forecasts.

The central result is a median forecast of \DwellingsMedian{} completed dwellings, with a 90\% interval of \DwellingsFive{}--\DwellingsNinetyFive{}. Because the government's 100,000-dwelling ambition lies above this interval, the panel considers that output level unlikely under current conditions. The permit forecast supports the same interpretation: the construction pipeline is not expected to expand sufficiently to resolve the structural shortage rapidly. The Rotterdam price and free-sector rent forecasts indicate that affordability pressure is also expected to persist.

These probabilistic judgments do not prove that the target cannot be reached or replace econometric forecasts; they make expert uncertainty explicit and empirically testable.

\section{Methodological Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

\textbf{Convenience panel.} The experts were recruited mainly through the researcher's network and are not representative of all relevant actors. Developers, lenders, municipalities, housing associations, and policymakers may hold different information; with only seven participants, individual responses also materially affect the pool.

\textbf{Remote elicitation.} Online completion prevented the researcher from observing misunderstandings or external-source use. Two patterns suggest residual unit errors. Retaining those values protects the scoring protocol but does not remove the measurement problem.

\textbf{Limited calibration sample.} With $n=10$, one realization changes a bin frequency by ten percentage points and the expected count in each 5\% tail bin is $0.5$. The asymptotic chi-square score is therefore a diagnostic rather than an exact finite-sample probability.

\textbf{Independence assumption.} Several seeds share macroeconomic drivers, so ten questions need not provide ten independent observations. A larger study could model dependence with copulas or vines~\cite{wernerEtAl2017}; this panel is too small to estimate such a structure credibly.

\textbf{Model specification.} Tested alternatives produced stable weights and forecasts, but cannot cover every defensible background measure or future structural break.

Future work should use a stratified panel, moderated interviews, more independent seeds, and validation against the eventual 2027 realizations.
